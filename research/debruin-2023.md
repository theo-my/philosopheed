## 2. Complete meta-ranking (Table 1 in the paper)

The paper's **Table 1** ("Meta-rankings and existing rankings of philosophy journals") lists **29 journals**. Every column is an *ordinal rank position* (1 = best), not a numeric score. The paper reports **four meta-ranking variants** side by side, plus five existing rankings for reference:

- **PCA** — aggregation via Principal Component Analysis (ex-post weighting). **This is the headline/primary meta-ranking and the column by which the table is sorted.**
- **AM** — aggregate rank by arithmetic mean of the induced ordinal ranks
- **HM** — aggregate rank by harmonic mean
- **Weighted** — ex-ante conceptual weighting across four metric categories
- Existing rankings (for comparison): **Leiter** (2018 "general" philosophy), **SJR** (Scimago), **CiteScore** (Scopus), **h5-index** (Google Scholar), **JNCI** (Web of Science Journal Normalized Citation Impact)

## 4. Notes and caveats

- **Multiple meta-ranking variants**: The paper does not give a single "the meta-ranking." Table 1 presents **four aggregation methods** (PCA, arithmetic mean, harmonic mean, ex-ante weighted). They are highly correlated (Spearman > 0.96 among the three means). PCA is used as the sort order of Table 1 and is treated here as the headline meta-ranking. If a different canonical order is preferred, AM (arithmetic mean) is the most conventional bibliometric choice; note it reorders the top 5 slightly (AM top 5: Noûs, Mind, PPR, Synthese, Philosophical Studies).
- **Values are ranks, not scores.** Each column is an ordinal position (1 = best). There is no underlying numeric quality score reported in Table 1.
- **Aggregation bias**: The author notes AM and HM carry "slightly different types of biases (towards low and high rankings, respectively)." Example given: Philosophical Quarterly's top-10 status depends on the choice of aggregation method.
- **Ties / cross-ranking artifacts**: In the source (existing) ranking columns—especially Leiter and JNCI—several journals share values and some values exceed 29 (up to 35), because those rankings were computed over a larger/different journal set. The four meta-ranking columns (PCA/AM/HM/Weighted) each run 1–29 with no ties.
- **A separate SURVEY ranking exists** (Table 5), distinct from this meta-ranking. It is de Bruin's own new survey (351 respondents, May 2022, over 40 general philosophy journals), reporting average quality assessments, "awareness," and awareness-adjusted quality. That is a different construct and is **not** transcribed here (the task asked for the meta-ranking). Table 4 shows the survey quality ranking is close to Leiter's but "rather different from the meta-ranking."
- **Journals excluded from the meta-ranking**: Some journals were dropped from Table 1 due to missing metrics; those excluded journals were re-included in the survey study (Table 5). The paper does not enumerate the excluded set within the meta-ranking table itself.
- **Philosophers' Imprint** is an online-only open-access journal — Wikipedia lists a single ISSN (1533-628X, treated as the electronic ISSN); no separate print ISSN.
- **ISSN/publisher data** for the top 20 are secondary-sourced (English Wikipedia infoboxes), not from the de Bruin paper, which does not list ISSNs. Publisher landscape can shift; verify against portal.issn.org before locking the dashboard config if absolute currency is required.
