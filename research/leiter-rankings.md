## 4. Methodology Notes

- **Poll type:** Reader polls run on Leiter Reports using pairwise/Condorcet-style voting (Condorcet Internet Voting Service, civs.us). Ranks derive from head-to-head "which journal publishes better work" comparisons, not raw scores.
- **Self-selected sample:** Voters are self-selected blog readers (skews Anglophone / analytic philosophy). Leiter himself flagged that "sophisticated Internet users" could vote multiple times; he reported results were nonetheless fairly stable across methods.
- **Participation:** General odds poll ~1,000 participants / ~90,000 comparisons; general Condorcet poll 479 votes; moral/political poll 329 votes.
- **"General" vs "specialist":** "General" journals publish across multiple areas (M&E, language, mind, science, value theory, history, logic). Specialist polls cover a single subfield.
- **Related but distinct — NOT a Leiter poll:** "A new ranking of philosophy journals, based on surveys and also 'objective' metrics" (Leiter Reports, 6 Dec 2023, https://leiterreports.com/2023/12/06/a-new-ranking-of-philosophy-journals-based-on-surveys-and-also-objective-metrics/) links a meta-ranking/survey study (published in *Synthese*, 2023) by other authors. It is not a Leiter reader poll and uses different methodology. Excluded from the canonical poll data above.
